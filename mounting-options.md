# Antenna Mounting Options

No-drill, no-roof, no-tower options for getting an antenna outside.

## Magnetic Mount Base
- Sticks to any flat metal surface (AC unit, metal railing, car roof, dryer vent hood)
- Built-in coax pigtail, usually SMA or BNC
- ~$10-15
- Search: "magnetic antenna mount SMA" or "mag mount BNC"
- Best for: whip antennas

## Suction Cup Window Mount
- Sticks to outside of a window pane
- Better for lighter/smaller antennas
- Search: "suction cup antenna mount"
- Best for: whip antennas, small dipoles

## Clamp / Jaw Mount
- Clamps onto railing, fence post, table edge, gutter, deck rail
- Very versatile, no permanent installation
- Search: "antenna jaw clamp mount" or "antenna pipe clamp"
- Best for: any antenna, most flexible option

## Camera Tripod + Adapter
- Cheap camera tripod with a 3/8" or 1/4" antenna adapter
- Set on porch, deck, yard, wherever
- Search: "antenna tripod adapter"
- Best for: quick setup/teardown, V-dipole satellite antenna

## DIY / Improvised
- Broomstick in a bucket of sand/gravel
- Zip-tied to a fence post
- Bungee cord to a porch column
- PVC pipe clamped to a railing

## V-Dipole Satellite Antenna (specific)
The 137 MHz V-dipole for NOAA/Meteor satellites points at the sky, not the horizon. It doesn't need height — it needs sky visibility. A small tripod or any flat surface with open sky works. Even laying it on a table on the porch pointed up is fine.
